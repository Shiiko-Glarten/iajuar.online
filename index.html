Hypermobility Syndrome

Definition: Hypermobility syndrome is characterized by excessive range of motion in joints, often associated with pain, instability, and injury.

Clinical Features

Key signs include:

  • Joint instability or frequent subluxations
  • Muscle fatigue or weakness
  • Chronic pain

Assessment

The Beighton Score is commonly used, focusing on joint hyperextension and flexibility benchmarks.

Management

Non-surgical: physiotherapy, strengthening, proprioceptive training.

Surgical: rarely indicated, reserved for severe instability.
